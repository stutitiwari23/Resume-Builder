# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming and inspiring community for all. We pledge to create an environment in which every individual feels valued and respected, regardless of background, identity, or experience level.

## Our Standards

**Examples of behavior that creates a positive environment include:**

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism
- Focusing on what is best for the community
- Showing empathy towards other community members
- Being patient and supportive of new contributors

**Examples of unacceptable behavior by participants include:**

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination of any kind
- Offensive comments related to gender, gender identity and expression, sexual orientation, disability, mental illness, neuro(a)typicality, physical appearance, body size, race, age, or religion
- Unwelcome sexual attention or comments
- Deliberate misgendering or use of "dead" or rejected names
- Trolling, insulting, or derogatory comments
- Threats of violence or incitement to violence of any kind
- Deliberate intimidation
- Sustained disruption of community spaces or discussions
- Publishing others' private information (doxxing)
- Spam or low-effort contributions intended to disrupt

## Enforcement

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying the standards of acceptable behavior and are expected to take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any instances of unacceptable behavior.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct, or to temporarily or permanently ban any contributor for other behaviors that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all community spaces (online and offline) and to all community members. This includes:

- GitHub repositories and discussions
- Issue trackers and pull requests
- Community forums and chat channels
- Project events and gatherings
- Professional spaces and communications

## Reporting Guidelines

If you encounter unacceptable behavior, please report it by:

1. **Emailing**: Include "CODE OF CONDUCT REPORT" in subject
2. **GitHub**: File an issue labeled `code-of-conduct-violation`
3. **Direct Contact**: Reach out to project maintainers privately

**When reporting, please provide:**

- Your contact information
- Names of individuals involved (if comfortable sharing)
- Description of the incident
- Context and any relevant links
- How the incident affected you

All reports will be reviewed and investigated. We commit to maintaining confidentiality where appropriate.

## Consequences

Violations of the Code of Conduct may result in:

1. **First Offense**: Warning and discussion about the behavior
2. **Repeated Offenses**: Temporary removal from community spaces
3. **Severe or Repeated Violations**: Permanent ban from the project

## Appeals

If you believe you have been unfairly sanctioned, you may appeal the decision to project leadership.
